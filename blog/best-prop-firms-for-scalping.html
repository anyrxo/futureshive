What Makes a Prop Firm Good for Scalping?

Not all prop firms are created equal when it comes to scalping. Here are the critical features scalpers need:

1. No Minimum Trade Duration

Some firms require positions to be held for a minimum time (30 seconds, 1 minute, etc.) to prevent "tick scalping" or latency arbitrage. For legitimate scalpers, you need firms with zero minimum hold time.

2. End-of-Day Drawdown vs Real-Time Daily Loss Limits

This is the most important distinction for scalpers:

  • End-of-Day (EOD) Drawdown: Calculated when the trading day closes. You can be down significantly intraday as long as you recover before market close. ‚úì BEST FOR SCALPERS
  • Real-Time Daily Loss Limit: If you hit the limit at any moment during the day, you immediately fail. ‚úó BAD FOR SCALPERS

üí° Why EOD Drawdown Matters for Scalpers

Scalpers take many small trades. Even with a 60% win rate, you'll have losing streaks. EOD drawdown gives you breathing room to recover from a bad morning with a profitable afternoon. Real-time daily limits can knock you out before you have a chance to adapt.

3. Low Commission Structure

When you're taking 20-50 trades per day, commissions matter more than profit splits:

  • ES Futures: $3.98-$5.00 per round trip
  • MES (Micro ES): $1.02-$1.42 per round trip
  • NQ Futures: $4.00-$5.00 per round trip

Example: 30 ES trades/day at $4/round trip = $120/day = $2,400/month in commissions. Choose firms with competitive commission structures.

4. News Trading Allowed

Many scalpers target volatility around news events (FOMC, NFP, CPI). Ensure your prop firm allows news trading without restrictions.

5. Fast Payout Processing

Active scalpers want quick access to profits. Look for firms with daily or on-demand payout options rather than monthly cycles.

EOD Drawdown vs Real-Time Daily Loss: The Critical Difference

Understanding this distinction is crucial for scalping success:

End-of-Day (EOD) Drawdown Example

My Funded Futures $50K Account: $1,250 EOD max loss

  • 9:30 AM: Start trading, account at $50,000
  • 11:00 AM: Hit a rough patch, down -$2,000 (account at $48,000)
  • Status: ‚úì STILL TRADING - because EOD limit isn't calculated until market close
  • 2:00 PM: Profitable afternoon session, recover to -$800 (account at $49,200)
  • 4:00 PM: End of day at -$800
  • Result: ‚úì PASS - within the $1,250 EOD limit

Real-Time Daily Loss Limit Example

FTMO $50K Account: $2,500 daily loss limit (real-time)

  • 9:30 AM: Start trading, account at $50,000

  • Status: ‚úó FAILED - you hit the daily loss limit
  • Result: Account closed immediately, even though you could have recovered by end of day

Real-time daily loss limits are account killers for scalpers. One bad streak in the morning and you're done, even if your strategy would have recovered by afternoon. Always choose EOD drawdown or no daily limit for scalping.

Tips for Passing Prop Firm Evaluations as a Scalper

1. Reduce Position Size During Evaluation

Trade smaller than you normally would. If you typically trade 3 ES contracts, drop to 1-2 during evaluation. The goal is to pass, not maximize profits.

2. Use Micro Contracts (MES, MNQ) for Testing

Micro E-mini contracts are 1/10th the size of standard contracts:

  • MES: $5/point vs ES $50/point
  • MNQ: $2/point vs NQ $20/point

Perfect for scalping evaluations with lower risk exposure and significantly lower commissions.

3. Track Your Intraday Drawdown

Even with EOD limits, monitor your largest intraday drawdown. If you're regularly hitting -$3,000 intraday on a $1,250 EOD limit, you're one bad session away from failing. Aim for max intraday drawdown of 2x your EOD limit.

4. Avoid Overleveraging During News

News events create volatility that scalpers love, but they also create risk:

  • Before NFP, CPI, FOMC: Reduce position size by 50%
  • During news release: Use wider stops (expect 10-20 point swings)
  • After news: Wait 5-10 minutes for volatility to normalize before resuming normal size

5. Take Breaks After Losing Streaks

Scalpers are prone to revenge trading after losses:

  • After 3 consecutive losses: Take a 15-minute break
  • After hitting 50% of EOD limit: Consider stopping for the day
  • After emotional trade: Immediate 30-minute break

6. Focus on High-Probability Setups During Evaluation

Don't scalp every tick movement during evaluation. Wait for:

  • Clear support/resistance levels
  • Volume confirmation
  • Trend alignment on higher timeframes (5-min, 15-min)

7. Use Demo Mode to Test EOD Drawdown Management

Before attempting evaluation, practice on demo with EOD limits:

  • Set a self-imposed EOD max loss (e.g., $1,250)
  • Track your max intraday drawdown
  • See if you can consistently stay within EOD limits for 2 weeks

Common Scalping Mistakes That Fail Prop Evaluations

Mistake #1: Overtrading Low-Probability Setups

The Problem: Scalping every 2-tick move without clear edge leads to death by a thousand cuts + commission costs.

The Solution: Define specific entry criteria. Example: "Only scalp when price bounces off VWAP with volume spike above 500 contracts."

Mistake #2: Not Accounting for Commissions

The Problem: Making $50 on a trade but paying $8 in commissions (2 contracts √ó $4) = net $42. After 50 trades, commissions eat $400.

The Solution: Target profit must be 3x commission cost minimum. For ES at $4 commission, target minimum $12/contract profit per trade.

Mistake #3: Scaling Up Too Fast After Wins

The Problem: Win 5 trades in a row scalping 1 ES contract, feel confident, jump to 4 contracts, lose next 2 trades, blow through EOD limit.

The Solution: Lock position size during evaluation. No increases until you pass and are funded.

Mistake #4: Ignoring Session Characteristics

The Problem: Trying to scalp during low-volume Asian session (6 PM - 12 AM ET) leads to poor fills and increased slippage.

The Solution: Focus on high-volume sessions:

  • 9:30 AM - 11:00 AM ET: NYSE open (highest volume)
  • 2:00 PM - 3:00 PM ET: Power hour
  • 8:30 AM ET: Economic news releases

Mistake #5: Holding Losing Scalps

The Problem: Enter scalp for 4-tick profit target, hits -3 ticks, "let me give it more room," becomes -10 tick loss.

The Solution: Scalps are defined by quick exits. Max hold time 2-5 minutes. If trade thesis breaks, exit immediately.

Frequently Asked Questions About Scalping Prop Firms

Which prop firm is best for scalping futures?

Apex Trader Funding is best for aggressive scalpers with its no daily loss limit policy, allowing unlimited intraday drawdown as long as you end the day within total drawdown limits.

My Funded Futures is best for disciplined scalpers who prefer end-of-day drawdown rules ($1,250-$5,000 EOD limits) that allow full intraday flexibility.

BluSky Trading is best for cost-conscious scalpers with low transaction fees and fast payout processing.

Do prop firms allow scalping?

Yes, most futures prop firms allow scalping, but with varying restrictions:

  • Apex, My Funded Futures, TopStep, BluSky Trading all permit scalping with no minimum trade duration
  • Take Profit Trader prohibits scalping and high-frequency trading
  • Some firms have faced issues with "aggressive scalping" that resembles latency arbitrage or tick scalping with excessive frequency

Always verify a firm's scalping policy before purchasing an evaluation.

What is end-of-day drawdown vs daily loss limit?

End-of-day (EOD) drawdown is calculated when the trading day closes, giving you full intraday flexibility.

Example: My Funded Futures $50K account has $1,250 EOD max loss - you can be down $2,000 intraday but must recover to -$1,250 or better by market close.

Daily loss limit is calculated in real-time throughout the day.

Example: FTMO $50K account has $2,500 daily loss limit - if you hit -$2,500 at any point during the day, you immediately fail, even if you could have recovered.

EOD drawdown is significantly better for scalpers because it allows recovery from large intraday drawdowns.

What are typical commission fees for scalping prop firms?

Commission fees for futures scalping vary by firm and product:

  • My Funded Futures: ES $4.26/round trip, MES $1.42/round trip, NQ $4.26/round trip
  • Apex Trader Funding: ES $3.98/round trip, MES $1.02-$1.04/round trip (varies by platform)
  • TopStep: Similar to live fees, approximately $4-5/round trip for ES, $1-2 for MES

For active scalpers taking 20-50 trades per day, commission costs add up quickly:

At $4/round trip, 30 ES trades per day = $120/day = $2,400/month in commissions.

Consider commission costs when calculating profitability - a 90% profit split with low commissions may be better than 100% split with high commissions.

Can I scalp during news events at prop firms?

Most prop firms allow news trading, but with caution:

  • TopStep: Allows news trading but discourages overleveraging during volatile events. Traders must still follow drawdown rules.
  • Apex: Permits news trading and holiday trading. No daily drawdown restrictions make it ideal for news scalpers.
  • My Funded Futures: Allows news trading. The EOD drawdown structure gives you flexibility to manage news volatility.
  • Take Profit Trader: Prohibits news scalping and has specific restricted trading windows.

Best practice for news scalpers:

  • Reduce position size during high-impact news (NFP, CPI, FOMC)
  • Use wider stops to avoid getting stopped out by volatility spikes
  • Ensure you have significant drawdown buffer before major news events
