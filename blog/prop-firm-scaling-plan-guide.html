What is a Prop Firm Scaling Plan?

A scaling plan is a structured path to increase your trading capital or position sizes after demonstrating consistent profitability and responsible risk management.

Two Types of Scaling

1. Account Size Scaling (FTMO, FundedNext, Earn2Trade)

Your actual account balance increases:

  • $50,000 ‚Üí $100,000 ‚Üí $200,000 ‚Üí $400,000+
  • Profit targets increase proportionally ($5K ‚Üí $10K ‚Üí $20K)
  • Drawdown limits increase (more room for larger trades)
  • Profit split percentages may improve (80% ‚Üí 90%)

2. Contract Scaling (TopStep, Apex)

Your account balance stays the same, but maximum position size increases:

  • Start: 2 contracts maximum
  • After $1,500 profit: 3 contracts
  • After $2,000 profit: 5 contracts
  • Account size remains $50K, but earning potential grows with more contracts

üí° Which is Better?

Account scaling is better for long-term wealth building. Doubling your account from $50K to $100K doubles your buying power and allows profits to compound faster.

Contract scaling gives quicker access to larger position sizes without waiting months for full account increases. Better for aggressive traders who want immediate earning potential.

Strategies to Scale Faster

Strategy #1: Target 3-5% Monthly Returns During Scaling Period

üí° The Sweet Spot

Most traders try to hit 10% per month to scale faster ‚Üí this leads to overtrading and blowing accounts.

Better approach: Target conservative 3-5% monthly returns with rock-solid consistency. This ensures:

  • You hit the 10% milestone reliably within 3-4 months
  • You maintain good trading metrics (consistency, drawdown control)
  • You build confidence in your strategy
  • You avoid blowing the account and restarting

Strategy #2: Request Scaling Exactly When Eligible

Don't wait. The moment you hit all requirements:

  1. Log into your prop firm dashboard
  2. Submit scaling request with performance summary
  3. Include trading journal showing consistency
  4. Emphasize drawdown control and risk management

Common mistake: Traders wait "to be safe" and continue trading on the smaller account for 6 more months, missing compounding opportunities.

Strategy #3: The "Multiple Firm" Approach

Instead of waiting 24 months to scale one FTMO account to $200K, use this strategy:

Month 0-3: Get First Funding

Pass FTMO $50K evaluation, get funded, start trading conservatively

Month 3-6: Add Second Firm

While trading FTMO $50K, pass TopStep $50K evaluation. Now trading 2 accounts = $100K combined

Month 6-12: First Scaling Milestone

FTMO $50K ‚Üí $100K. TopStep scales contracts. Combined allocation: $150K+

Month 12-18: Add Third Account or Scale Further

FTMO hits $200K, TopStep at max contracts, consider adding Apex $150K. Total: $350K+ across 3 firms

Month 18-24: $400K+ Allocation

FTMO $200K + TopStep $150K + Apex $150K = $500K combined allocation in 2 years vs waiting 4+ years for one firm to scale to $500K

‚ö†Ô∏è Important: Risk Management with Multiple Accounts

Never trade correlated strategies across multiple accounts. If you're trading the same ES strategy on 3 accounts, one bad market day can wipe all three simultaneously.

Better approach:

  • Account 1 (FTMO): ES day trading
  • Account 2 (TopStep): NQ scalping
  • Account 3 (Apex): Swing trading overnight positions

This diversification protects your income stream if one strategy underperforms.

Strategy #4: Optimize Payout Frequency

Balance between taking payouts (required for scaling) and leaving capital in account to hit profit targets faster:

Example on FTMO $50K:

  • Month 1: Earn $2,500 (5%), withdraw $1,500 (first payout), keep $1,000 in account
  • Month 2: Earn $2,000 more (total $3,000), withdraw $1,500 (second payout), keep $1,500 in account
  • Month 3: Earn $3,000 more (total $6,000), withdraw $1,500 (third payout), keep $4,500 in account
  • Month 4: Final push to $10,000 total profit (10% milestone hit!)

Result: You got 3 required payouts ($4,500 total withdrawn) while still accumulating the $10,000 profit needed for scaling. Request scaling in Month 4-5 instead of Month 8-10.

Common Scaling Mistakes That Delay Growth

Mistake #1: Overtrading to Hit Milestones Faster

The Problem: Trader has $7,500 profit on FTMO $50K after 2 months. Gets impatient, starts taking 20 trades per day to hit $10,000 faster. Blows $3,000 in one bad week, drops to $4,500. Now has to start over.

The Solution: Stick to your proven strategy. If you're earning 3-5% per month consistently, you'll hit milestones on schedule without risk.

Mistake #2: Not Requesting Scaling When Eligible

The Problem: Trader hits all requirements (10% profit, 3 payouts, 90 days), but doesn't request scaling for 4 more months "just to be safe."

The Cost: 4 months of trading on $50K instead of $100K = missed $2,000-4,000 in additional profits from larger position sizes.

The Solution: Submit scaling request the moment you're eligible. Worst case, they ask for another month. Best case, you're scaled immediately.

Mistake #3: Taking Too Many Payouts Too Soon

The Problem: Trader withdraws every dollar earned. Earns $1,000, withdraws $1,000. Earns $800, withdraws $800. After 6 months, has $10,000 in payouts but account shows $0 net profit.

Why It Matters: Firms base scaling on profit remaining in account, not total payouts. You need to show $10,000 cumulative profit for next scale.

The Solution: Leave at least 50-60% of profits in account until you hit scaling milestone, then withdraw.

Mistake #4: Scaling Before You're Ready

The Problem: Trader gets lucky, hits 10% profit in 6 weeks with 70% from one trade. Requests scaling, gets approved, immediately loses $5,000 on $100K account because strategy isn't truly consistent.

The Solution: Even if you hit profit target, wait until you have 3-6 months of consistent performance. If you can't consistently earn 3-5% monthly on $50K, you'll struggle even more on $100K.
