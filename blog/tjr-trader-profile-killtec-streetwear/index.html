TJR's Trading Approach: Futures Markets

TJR primarily trades futures markets, with a focus on major indices. His approach combines technical analysis with an understanding of market structure and liquidity.

Why Futures Trading?

TJR chose futures for several strategic reasons:

  • Leverage: Futures contracts allow significant leverage, enabling larger position sizes with smaller capital requirements
  • Liquidity: Major futures contracts (ES, NQ) have exceptional liquidity, ensuring tight spreads and easy entry/exit
  • Market Hours: Futures trade nearly 24/5, providing flexibility for different schedules
  • Tax Benefits: 60/40 tax treatment in the US (60% long-term, 40% short-term capital gains)

TJR's Trading Methodology

While TJR doesn't publicly disclose his complete trading system, observations from his live trading sessions reveal several key principles:

1. Focus on Price Action

TJR emphasizes clean price action over indicator-heavy charts. His approach includes:

  • Identifying key support and resistance levels
  • Trading breakouts and breakdowns with confirmation
  • Using volume analysis to confirm moves
  • Respecting major psychological price levels

2. Risk-First Mentality

Every trade begins with identifying:

  • Invalidation point: Where the trade idea is wrong
  • Position size: Calculated based on distance to stop loss
  • Risk-reward ratio: Minimum 2:1, often targeting 3:1 or better

3. Session-Based Trading

TJR focuses on specific market sessions when volatility and volume are highest:

  • 9:30 AM - 11:30 AM ET: NYSE open session (highest volume)
  • 2:00 PM - 4:00 PM ET: Power hour and close
  • Avoiding choppy mid-day sessions when possible
