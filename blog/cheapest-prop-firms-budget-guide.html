Understanding Refundable Fee Policies

A refundable fee policy means the firm returns your initial evaluation fee once you successfully pass the challenge and complete your first profit withdrawal. Here's how it works across different firms:

How Refunds Work

Step 1: You pay $22 for Maven Trading's $5K evaluation

Step 2: You pass the 2-step evaluation by hitting profit targets within risk limits

Step 3: You trade the funded account and request your first payout (minimum typically $50-$100)

Step 4: The firm includes your $22 evaluation fee with your first payout

Result: Your evaluation was effectively free

Important notes about refunds:

  • One-time refund: Most firms refund the fee only once, typically with your first withdrawal
  • Must pass evaluation: If you fail and reset, the reset fee is usually NOT refundable
  • Minimum payout requirements: You must meet the firm's minimum withdrawal amount (usually $50-$100)
  • Account must remain active: Some firms require ongoing activity or monthly fees to maintain funded status

Firms like TopStep automatically refund an amount equivalent to your most recent monthly subscription fee when you request your first profit payout, occurring only once per participant.

Reset Costs: What Happens If You Fail?

If you exceed the maximum loss limit during your evaluation, you'll breach the account. Most firms offer a reset option to give you another attempt without purchasing a completely new challenge.

The Trade-Offs of Cheapest Prop Firms

While budget prop firms offer incredible accessibility, they typically come with stricter rules and limitations compared to premium providers. Understanding these trade-offs helps you set realistic expectations.

1. Tighter Drawdown Limits

Budget firms often enforce stricter risk controls:

  • Daily Loss Limits: 3-5% (vs. 5-8% at premium firms)
  • Total Drawdown: 4-8% (vs. 8-12% at premium firms)
  • Impact: Less room for error, requires tighter stop-losses and smaller position sizes

Example: On a $5K Maven Trading account with 5% daily limit, you can only lose $250 in a single day before breaching. Compare this to FTMO's 5% on a $10K account ($500 daily loss allowed).

2. Lower Leverage Caps

Most cheap prop firms cap leverage at 1:30 to 1:50, while premium firms offer 1:100+. The exception is ThinkCapital at 1:100.

  • Lower Leverage Impact: Requires more capital to open same-sized positions
  • Who It Affects: Scalpers and day traders who rely on high leverage
  • Benefit: Reduced risk of over-leveraging and margin calls

3. Limited Scaling Opportunities

Budget firms often max out scaling at $50K-$100K, compared to $400K+ at top-tier firms.

Getting Started with a Budget Prop Firm

Follow these steps to maximize your chances of passing a cheap prop firm evaluation:

  1. Start with your proven strategy: Don't try new approaches during evaluations
  2. Demo trade first: Practice the exact profit targets and risk limits on a demo for 2-4 weeks
  3. Calculate position sizes: Use 0.5-1% risk per trade to stay well under daily limits
  4. Set daily profit goals: For a $5K account targeting $400 Phase 1 profit, aim for $30-40 per day over 10-12 days
  5. Track every metric: Daily P&L, drawdown percentage, number of trades, win rate
  6. Stop trading after hitting limits: Hit 80% of your daily limit? Done for the day.
  7. Plan your first payout: Know the minimum withdrawal amount and timing before you start

With the right preparation, a $23-$37 investment can unlock access to $5K-$10K in trading capital and scale to six-figure accounts over time.
