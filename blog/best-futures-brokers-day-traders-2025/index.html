Frequently Asked Questions

Which broker has the lowest futures commissions?
Interactive Brokers offers the lowest futures commissions at $0.25 per contract for most futures (ES, NQ, CL, GC). However, you need to execute 10,000+ contracts per month to qualify for this rate. For traders with lower volume, NinjaTrader Brokerage offers $0.53/contract with no volume requirements, and Tradovate offers $0.85/contract with a free platform. When comparing, also factor in data fees ($4-15/month per exchange) and platform costs.
What is the best futures broker for beginners?
TD Ameritrade (thinkorswim) is the best futures broker for beginners because: 1) Free, user-friendly thinkorswim platform with excellent education resources, 2) No minimum deposit required, 3) PaperMoney simulator for risk-free practice, 4) 24/7 customer support, 5) Comprehensive tutorials and webinars. The trade-off is higher commissions ($2.25/contract vs $0.25-$0.85 at other brokers). Once you're consistently profitable and trading higher volume, transition to Interactive Brokers or NinjaTrader for lower costs.
Do I need a lot of money to start trading futures?
Minimum capital depends on the broker and contract: Day trading margin (intraday) for ES futures is typically $500-$1,380 per contract, NQ futures $1,000-$2,100 per contract. However, professional traders recommend 3-5x the minimum margin for proper risk management. For beginners, start with Micro E-mini futures (MES/MNQ) which require only $50-$300 margin, allowing you to trade with $500-$1,000 total capital. Most brokers have no account minimum, but $2,500-$5,000 is recommended for realistic futures trading.
What is the difference between futures brokers and prop firms?
Futures brokers provide access to exchanges using YOUR capital (you deposit money, trade with your own funds, keep 100% of profits). Prop firms provide THEIR capital after you pass an evaluation (you pay evaluation fee, trade with firm's money, keep 80-90% of profits). Use brokers when you have sufficient capital ($5,000+) and want full control. Use prop firms when you have limited capital but proven skills - they provide up to $300K buying power after passing challenges. Many professional traders use both: personal broker account for strategies + prop firm accounts for scaling.
What are data fees for futures trading?
Data fees are monthly subscriptions for real-time exchange data, required for live trading. CME Group data (ES, NQ, CL, GC): $4-15/month depending on broker bundles. Most brokers offer: Professional data: $100-150/month for all exchanges. Non-professional (retail) data: $15-30/month for CME + NYMEX. Delayed data: Free (15-20 minute delay, unusable for day trading). Many brokers waive data fees if you trade 30-50+ contracts per month. Budget $15-30/month for data fees when starting out.
Can I trade futures with NinjaTrader for free?
Yes, NinjaTrader platform is completely free with any supported broker connection (including NinjaTrader Brokerage, Interactive Brokers, TD Ameritrade). You only pay for: 1) Real-time data feeds ($4-15/month per exchange), 2) Trading commissions ($0.53-$2.25/contract depending on broker), 3) Optional add-ons like advanced order flow tools. The platform itself has no monthly fee, unlike competitors (Sierra Chart $36/month, Quanttower Premium $70/month). This makes NinjaTrader excellent for cost-conscious traders who want professional-grade software.
Which futures broker is best for automated trading?
NinjaTrader Brokerage and Interactive Brokers are best for automated trading. NinjaTrader offers: Free platform with C# programming for custom strategies, extensive strategy builder (no coding required), 1,000+ third-party indicators and strategies, robust backtesting engine, and $0.53/contract commissions. Interactive Brokers offers: Powerful API for algorithmic trading (Python, C++, Java), lowest commissions ($0.25/contract), institutional-grade infrastructure, and multi-asset automation. Choose NinjaTrader for futures-only automation with visual tools. Choose Interactive Brokers for cross-asset algorithms with coding.
Are there any hidden fees with futures brokers?
Yes, watch for these hidden fees: 1) Inactivity fees ($10-50/month if you don't trade), 2) Platform fees ($100-300/month for professional software like CQG), 3) Data fees ($15-150/month depending on exchanges), 4) Exchange fees ($1.20-2.00 per contract on top of commission), 5) NFA regulatory fees ($0.02 per contract), 6) Overnight/position fees (some brokers charge for holding overnight), 7) Withdrawal fees ($25-50 for wires). Always calculate TOTAL cost per round-turn (entry + exit), not just advertised commission. A '$0.25' commission broker might cost $2.50 total with all fees included.
